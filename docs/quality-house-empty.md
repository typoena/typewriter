# Quality House — empty (training)

The same **four-house cascade** as [`qfd.md`](qfd.md), with every scored
surface deliberately blank. Fill each house in yourself, carry your
derived Σ down to the next house's importance column, then compare against
the populated houses ([House 1 in §3](qfd-house-1.md#3-house-of-quality--whats--hows),
[House 2 in §5](qfd-house-2.md#5-how--component-mapping-phase-2), [Houses 3–4](qfd-houses-3-4.md)) to check your
reading.

Kept (definitional inputs from the scored `qfd-*.md` houses):

- WHATs + importance weights (§1) and HOWs + v0.1 targets (§2) — House 1
- The component (C1–C20), process (P1–P9), and control (Q1–Q8) catalogues
  (§5) — Houses 2–4 row/column labels

Blank (training surface):

- All four relation matrices and roofs
- All four basements (Σ / Rel % / Rank)
- The importance columns of Houses 2–4 — carrying your own derived Σ down
  the cascade **is** the exercise
- Perception zone (hidden, not part of the exercise)

## How to fill it in

1. **House 1 relations (centre).** For each WHAT × HOW cell where the HOW
   contributes to the WHAT, drop a marker:
   ```
   \node[qfdrel/S] at ({COL - 0.5}, {-ROW + 0.5}) {};   % strong = 9
   \node[qfdrel/M] at ({COL - 0.5}, {-ROW + 0.5}) {};   % medium = 3
   \node[qfdrel/W] at ({COL - 0.5}, {-ROW + 0.5}) {};   % weak   = 1
   ```
   Leave a cell empty for "no relation."
2. **Roof.** For each pair of columns that reinforce or conflict:
   ```
   \node[font=\scriptsize] at (C-i-j) {$+\!+$};   % i < j
   ```
   Symbols: `$+\!+$` very positive, `$+$` positive, `$-$` negative,
   `$-\!-$` very negative. Leave a slot empty for "independent."
3. **House 1 basement.** Per HOW column, Σ abs = sum of
   `(importance × cell strength)` over all WHATs (strength 9 / 3 / 1 / 0);
   Rel % = Σ ÷ total × 100 (integer, sums to ~100).
4. **Carry it down.** Your House-1 basement Σ becomes House 2's row
   importance. Score HOWs × components the same way; the basement is the
   **derived component Σ** (row importance × strength) + rank. Parenthesise
   and leave unranked any component that isn't actually built — scored
   fiction outranks real components.
5. **Houses 3–4.** Same again: House 2's derived Σ → House 3's row
   importance (components × processes), House 3's rel-% → House 4's
   (processes × controls). From House 3 on, record rel-% + rank rather
   than raw Σ — the absolute numbers grow geometrically and stop being
   readable.
6. **Diff.** Compare your basements and roofs against
   [`qfd-house-1.md` §3](qfd-house-1.md#3-house-of-quality--whats--hows) and
   [`qfd-house-2.md` §5](qfd-house-2.md#5-how--component-mapping-phase-2) — divergences
   are usually the most interesting part.

## Gotchas

- `<` and `>` render as `¡` `¿` in node text — use `$<$`, `$>$`, `$-$`.
- Row 1 is the top row; column 1 is the leftmost column.
- The perception zone is hidden here on purpose. To bring it back in
  House 1, delete the two `\qfdshow...false` lines near the top of its
  block.
- Houses 2–4 share House 1's preamble verbatim (as in the `qfd-*.md` houses): a style
  change to one block must be pasted into all of them.
